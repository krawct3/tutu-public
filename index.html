About Twirling Princess

ballet magic in the Bay Area

Twirling Princess is a beloved dance program offering Tutu School's acclaimed ballet curriculum to young children ages 2–8 across four Bay Area studios. We believe every child deserves to know what it feels like to dance — to move with joy, build confidence, and discover the magic of music and movement in a warm, nurturing environment.

Our small class sizes ensure every child receives individual attention and encouragement. Our faculty are trained in Tutu School's developmentally appropriate curriculum, and our studios are designed to feel beautiful, cozy, and full of just enough sparkle to feel truly magical.

About Tutu School

our curriculum and national network

Twirling Princess is a proud franchisee of Tutu School — a nationally recognized early childhood dance program with studios across the United States. Tutu School's Ballet Storytime curriculum brings classical ballets to life while promoting positive values like kindness, bravery, and imagination. Every class is thoughtfully designed for where young dancers are developmentally, making their first experience with ballet magical and meaningful.

About the Owner

locally owned, community focused

Sonya Krawczyk

Owner, Twirling Princess · Tutu School Franchisee

Sonya's connection to Tutu School began the way it does for so many families — through her daughter. After witnessing the joy and growth her child experienced week after week in class, she knew she had found something truly special. With years of experience in business operations, she made the leap to bring that same magic to children across the Bay Area.

Today, Sonya operates four Twirling Princess studios across San Francisco, Berkeley, Hercules, and Daly City. She is committed to maintaining high-quality, well-trained faculty, fostering Tutu School's curriculum of creativity and joy, and engaging meaningfully with the families and communities each studio serves.

Sonya grew up with fond memories of attending ballet and music lessons with her mother — and is proud to help create those same lasting memories for a new generation of Bay Area children.
